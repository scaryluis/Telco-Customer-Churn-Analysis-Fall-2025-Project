# Telco Customer Churn Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes customer churn behavior using a telecom dataset of 7,000+ customers. The goal was to identify key factors influencing customer retention and provide data-driven recommendations to reduce churn.

## Objective

* Identify drivers of customer churn
* Analyze the relationship between pricing, tenure, and customer behavior
* Support business decisions related to retention and revenue

## Tools & Skills

* Microsoft Excel
* Descriptive Statistics
* Hypothesis Testing (t-test)
* Correlation Analysis
* Regression Analysis
* Data Visualization

## Dataset

The dataset includes:

* Customer demographics (e.g., gender, senior citizen status)
* Tenure (length of time as a customer)
* MonthlyCharges
* TotalCharges
* Churn status

## Key Insights

* Customers who churn pay ~21% higher monthly charges on average ($74 vs. $61), indicating pricing sensitivity
* Customer tenure explains ~68% of total revenue variation, making it a strong predictor of long-term value
* Monthly charges show minimal relationship with tenure, suggesting pricing does not scale with customer loyalty

## Business Recommendations

* Introduce targeted discounts or pricing adjustments to reduce churn among high-paying customers
* Improve onboarding and early-stage engagement to retain new customers longer
* Focus retention efforts on high-value, long-tenure customers

## Project Deliverables

* Statistical analysis (t-test, regression)
* Excel-based data exploration
* Business insights and recommendations report

## Outcome

This analysis highlights pricing and tenure as key drivers of churn and provides actionable strategies to improve customer retention and long-term revenue.
